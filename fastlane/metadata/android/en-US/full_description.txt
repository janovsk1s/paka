Paka is an intentionally small, offline pass-and-authenticator tool designed for the Light Phone III. It scans and renders common barcode formats, carries encrypted PDF and photo passes, and generates TOTP codes without Google Play Services.

<b>Private by construction</b>

<ul>
<li>No internet permission, no analytics, no advertising.</li>
<li>Camera access is requested only while scanning.</li>
<li>Pass data and TOTP secrets are encrypted with separate AES-256-GCM keys held by the Android Keystore.</li>
<li>Imported PDFs and photos are stored only as ciphertext; viewing decrypts them into RAM, never a plaintext file.</li>
<li>Android cloud backup and device transfer are disabled. Portable backups are encrypted and authenticated offline with a key derived from your passphrase.</li>
<li>TOTP codes copied to the clipboard are marked sensitive and cleared after 30 seconds.</li>
</ul>

<b>Quiet by design</b>

Paka follows the restrained, monochrome character of the Light Phone III: a pure black background, five list entries per page, hard-cut page transitions, and no ripples or animation flourishes. Barcodes are decoded and payload-checked again before display, so the symbol on screen always matches what was scanned.

Paka is an independent, unofficial community tool. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, sponsored by, or published by The Light Phone, Inc.
